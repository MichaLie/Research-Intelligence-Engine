## Notes

### Evidence Patterns (Round 1)

1. **Convergent finding**: Multiple independent studies consistently show that unmodified EVs accumulate predominantly in liver/spleen after IV injection, with brain uptake <2% of injected dose. This is the most robust finding in the evidence base.

2. **Mechanism debate**: Adsorptive transcytosis (Banks 2020) vs. receptor-mediated transcytosis (Matsumoto 2017, Ramos-Zaldivar 2022) may not be contradictory — different EV subtypes likely use different pathways.

3. **Inflammation confound**: Studies showing increased brain uptake during inflammation (Banks 2020, Yuan 2017, Perets 2019) cannot clearly distinguish between specific transcytosis upregulation and nonspecific BBB disruption.

4. **In vitro model discrepancy**: Morad 2019 shows transcytosis in a sophisticated 3D model; Klepe 2026 shows no crossing in a transwell model. Model differences (flow, cell types, complexity) likely explain this, but it undermines confidence in in vitro findings generally.

5. **Publication bias concern**: Negative results like Klepe 2026 are rare in this literature. The evidence base may be systematically biased toward positive brain delivery findings.

6. **No systematic reviews**: Despite the maturity of this field, no systematic review with meta-analysis of quantitative brain uptake data has been published.

### Key Conflicts

- Morad 2019 (EVs cross intact BBB) vs. Klepe 2026 (EVs do not detectably cross BBB transwell)
- Adsorptive transcytosis (Banks 2020) vs. receptor-mediated pathways (Matsumoto 2017)
- Natural EV brain targeting (Ramos-Zaldivar 2022: "occurs even under healthy conditions") vs. negligible brain uptake (Wiklander 2015, Smyth 2015)

### Gaps

- No quantification of transcytotic efficiency (endocytosed fraction that completes transcytosis)
- No systematic comparison of BBB vs. CVO/choroid plexus entry routes
- No single-EV in vivo tracking
- Brain-to-blood export mechanism unknown
- No meta-analysis of quantitative brain uptake data

### Evidence Patterns (Round 2)

7. **Choroid plexus as signal transducer**: Round 2 transformed the CP/CVO evidence gap. Five studies establish that the CP possesses exosome transcytosis machinery (Grapp 2013), actively generates EVs in response to peripheral inflammation (Balusu 2016), and is accessible to circulating nanoparticles (Millward 2013, Wang 2025). The CP acts as a transducer (sensing peripheral signals, generating brain-directed EVs) rather than a passive conduit for circulating EVs.

8. **L1CAM controversy is genuine and unresolved**: Norman 2021 (Nature Methods) and Kadam 2025 show L1CAM is predominantly soluble in biofluids. Nogueras-Ortiz 2024 shows L1CAM IS on individual EVs by single-EV analysis. Gomes & Witwer 2022 systematic review attributes the discrepancy to methodological heterogeneity. You 2023 proposes ATP1A3 as a more specific alternative marker.

9. **Exosome depletion produces brain phenotypes, but mechanism is brain-local**: GW4869 and nSMASE2 heterozygosity reduce amyloid pathology (Dinkins 2014, 2016). However, all effects are attributable to brain-intrinsic exosome signaling. No study has tested peripheral-specific EV depletion.

10. **PBPK models for EVs are at framework stage only**: Kumar 2023 proposes framework; Azeez 2025 confirms no validated model exists for EV brain delivery. Steady-state endogenous EV exposure at BBB is unquantified.

11. **Glycocalyx is a critical overlooked barrier**: Walter 2021, Santa-Maria 2021, and Shi 2025 (Nature) establish the glycocalyx as a negatively charged physical pre-filter that requires flow for formation, is absent in static transwells, and degrades with aging/disease. This may explain both the low in vivo brain uptake and the discrepancy between in vitro and in vivo results.

12. **Cargo delivery without transcytosis is plausible but untested**: Toth 2020, Joshi & Zuhorn 2021, and O'Brien 2020 provide mechanistic support for the ICD model (H6), but no direct experimental test at the BBB exists.

### Key Conflicts (Updated)

- Morad 2019 (EVs cross intact BBB) vs. Klepe 2026 (EVs do not detectably cross BBB transwell) -- now partially resolved by glycocalyx/flow explanation (Santa-Maria 2021)
- Norman 2021/Kadam 2025 (L1CAM is soluble) vs. Nogueras-Ortiz 2024 (L1CAM is on individual EVs) -- methodological differences explain discrepancy
- GW4869/nSMASE2 brain phenotypes: exosome depletion vs. ceramide pathway disruption -- cannot be distinguished with current tools

### Gaps (Updated Round 2)

- Peripheral-specific EV depletion experiment (liver-specific nSMASE2 KO or parabiosis)
- EV-endothelial transcriptomic response at BBB (test for ICD model H6)
- Glycocalyx-EV direct interaction biophysics
- Validated PBPK model for EV brain delivery under steady-state conditions
- In vivo validation of ATP1A3 or other alternative neuronal EV markers
